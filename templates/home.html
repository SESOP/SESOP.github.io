Secondary Energy

Special Opportunities Partnership

The Secondary Energy Special Opportunities Partnership (SESOP) has been established to make equity and equity related investments in two areas of the alternative energy value chain in South Africa, namely solar and wind power. With the dynamics of the energy sector changing rapidly over the past few years, this has led to amazing opportunities in the alternative energy space.

The Partnership has a strong, agile and well-balanced team consisting of seasoned executives, industry experts and investment professionals, with a combined career spanning over 140 years. Over time, the Partnership expects to bolster its team further.

Energy Sector

Power Supply vs Demand

The South African Electricity Sector has been receiving significant attention over the past several years due to continued blackouts. Local demand for electricity has grown rapidly over the past few years in South Africa and continues to do so. This demand has been spurred on by economic and population growth, which have out grown the growth rate of electricity supply. However, projections suggest that by 2025 this needs to reach 80,000 MW. With energy supply constantly under pressure and blackouts expected to continue into 2015, there is an urgent need for change in the energy sector.

The Integrated Resources Plan (IPR) was finalised in March 2011, with the intention of creating an additional 52,248MW of new power producing capacity. The introduction of Independent Power Producers (IPP) has been included to meet long term demand for electricity in South Africa. This has created opportunities for private investors to get involved in the alternative energy space.
